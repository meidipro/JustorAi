Bangladesh Labour Act, 2006
(Act No. 42 of 2006)
[11 October 2006]

An Act to consolidate and amend the laws relating to employment of workers, relations between workers and employers, determination of minimum wages, payment of wages, compensation for injuries to workers during the course of their employment, formation of trade unions, raising and settlement of industrial disputes, health, safety, welfare and working conditions of workers, and apprenticeship and matters ancillary thereto.

Whereas it is expedient and necessary to consolidate and amend the laws relating to employment of workers, relations between workers and employers, determination of minimum wages, payment of wages, compensation for injuries to workers during the course of their employment, formation of trade unions, raising and settlement of industrial disputes, health, safety, welfare and working conditions of workers, and apprenticeship and matters ancillary thereto;

It is hereby enacted as follows:-

Chapter I
Preliminary

1. Short title, commencement and application.—(1) This Act may be called the Bangladesh Labour Act, 2006.
(2) It shall come into force at once.
(3) Unless anything otherwise is provided in this Act, it shall apply to the whole of Bangladesh.
(4) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (3), this Act shall not apply to the following establishments or workers, namely:
(a) any office of or under the control of the Government;
(b) Security Printing Press;
(c) ordinance factories;
(d) any establishment run for the treatment, care or service of sick, infirm, old, destitute, disabled, orphan, abandoned women or children or widows, but not run for profit or gain;
(e) any shop or stall in a public exhibition for the purpose of which it is so established, where only retail sale is carried on;
(f) any shop or stall in a public fair or market for religious or charitable purposes;
(g) any educational, training or research institution not run for profit or gain; (Amended in 2013)
(h) any hostel or mess not run for profit or gain, "hospital, clinic and diagnostic centre"; (Amended in 2013)
(i) any shop or industrial or commercial establishment owned by the Government and run directly by the Government, where the workers are governed by the conduct rules applicable to Government employees;
(j) any worker whose employment and terms of service are governed by any Act or rules made under Article 62, 79, 113 or 133 of the Constitution, provided that for the purpose of Chapters XII, XIII and XIV, this Act shall apply to them.

2. Definitions.—In this Act, unless there is anything repugnant in the subject or context-
(1) "retirement" means the termination of service of a worker on attaining a certain age as per section 28 of this Act. However, voluntary retirement of a worker from any establishment after completing 25 years of service shall also be deemed as retirement;
(1a) "partial disablement" means, where the disablement is of a temporary nature, such disablement as reduces his earning capacity in respect of every employment in which he was engaged at the time of the accident resulting in the disablement, and, where the disablement is of a permanent nature, such disablement as reduces his earning capacity in respect of every such employment: Provided that every injury specified in the first Schedule shall be deemed to result in permanent partial disablement;
(2) "manufacturing process" means any of the following processes, namely: (a) making, altering, repairing, ornamenting, finishing, packing, oiling, washing, cleaning, breaking up, demolishing or otherwise treating any article or substance with a view to its use, sale, transport, delivery or disposal, (b) pumping oil, gas, water, sewage or any other liquid or sullage, (c) generating, transforming or transmitting power or gas, (d) constructing, reconstructing, repairing, finishing or breaking up ships or vessels, (e) printing by letterpress, lithography, photogravure, computer, photo composing, offset or other like process or book-binding which is carried on by way of trade or for purposes of gain or incidentally to another business so carried on;
(2a) "festival allowance" means the festival allowance determined by rules payable to the workers in any factory or establishment before their respective religious festivals; (Inserted by 2018 amendment)
